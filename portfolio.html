Week 2

The second task was to build two live charts and embed them into my webpage. Both charts relate to IMF lending, with the second chart correcting for the failures of the first chart.

IMF lending (in Nominal terms)

The first chart shows the nominal value of IMF lending to all members been 1984-2022. However, in order to make our data more applicable, it needs to be transformed.

IMF Lending as a % of World GDP

The second chart builds on the previous chart by dividing this nominal value of lending by global GDP, which helps present our raw data in a much better manner.

This chart shows that the IMF lent the most in 1984 (as a proportion of Global GDP), thus suggesting this was the riskiest year to invest in markets.

Notably, from the second chart it is clear that IMF lending (relative to GDP) followed a downward trajectory after 2000. This clearly contrasts to the message portrayed by the first graph, which showed that IMF credit payments increased in the same time frame. This validates why the data transformation was required.
